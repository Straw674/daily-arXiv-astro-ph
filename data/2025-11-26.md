### [66] [Astronomical Methods and Instrumentation in the Islamic World: Past, Present, Future](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.19559)
*Armin MAghami Asl,Yaseen Almleaky*

**TL;DR**: 研究梳理了伊斯兰天文学（Islamic astronomy）从黄金时代（8-15世纪）的观测理论创新、到衰落期（15-17世纪）的技术滞后、再到当代复兴的三阶段发展轨迹，揭示了其作为全球科学史关键纽带的历史作用及当前通过区域合作重返天体物理学前沿的潜力。

Background: 伊斯兰天文学（Islamic astronomy）研究跨越千年传统，涵盖黄金时代（8至15世纪）的创新传承、衰落期（15至17世纪）的停滞因素以及当代复兴阶段（如观测合作与太空计划），在全球科学史中扮演了连接数学严谨性、观测方法与星名体系的关键角色。

Data: 数据来源包括历史文献记载（如al-Sufi手稿）、现代天文台合作观测数据、以及参与国（摩洛哥、伊朗、土耳其、阿联酋和沙特阿拉伯）新兴太空计划获取的卫星遥感数据。

Method: 采用历史比较分析法（comparative historical analysis）梳理伊斯兰天文学发展的三阶段特征，并评估其与中欧传统的知识传递作用。

Result: 伊斯兰天文学（Islamic astronomy）通过三个历史阶段（黄金时代、衰落期和当代复兴）展现了其在全球科学史中的关键纽带作用，并预示了其在现代天体物理学中重新贡献的潜力。

Abstract: From al-Sufi's tenth-century observation of the Andromeda Galaxy as a "little cloud" to contemporary space missions, Islamic astronomy represents a millennium-spanning tradition of innovation and knowledge. This study traces its trajectory through three phases: the Golden Age (8th to 15th centuries), when scholars such as al-Biruni, al-Battani, and Ibn Sina developed instruments, cataloged the heavens, and refined theories that later influenced Copernicus; a period of decline (late 15th to 17th centuries), shaped by political fragmentation, economic shifts, and the delayed adoption of technologies such as printing and the telescope; and today's revival, marked by observatory collaborations, Olympiad successes, and emerging space programs in Morocco, Iran, Turkey, the UAE, and Saudi Arabia. This comparative analysis with Chinese and European scientific traditions shows how Islamic astronomy provided a vital link in the global history of science, transmitting mathematical rigor, observational methods, and Arabic star names that are still used today. The contemporary resurgence signals the potential for renewed contributions to astrophysics, provided that it is supported by regional observatory networks, space-based research initiatives, and educational frameworks that integrate historical heritage with modern computational science.
